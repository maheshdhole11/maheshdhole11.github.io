Welcome

I am a Ph.D. candidate in the Department of Economics at Southern Illinois University Carbondale, expecting to graduate in May 2027.

My research lies at the intersection of labor economics, automation and technological change, development economics, and monetary theory. I am particularly interested in how public policies—minimum wages and state tax regimes—shape the impact of technological change on workers' earnings, job security, and inequality.

I am on the 2026–27 economics job market.
My job market paper examines whether high minimum wages insulate or expose workers to the effects of technological change.

Before my Ph.D., I worked for nearly a decade as a civil engineer and project manager in India.
